Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 1, 2026   |   Last Updated: February 20, 2026

1. Introduction

MirrorMind is an academic AI‑powered smart classroom monitoring and analytics platform developed for educational institutions. This Privacy Policy describes how the platform processes information when students and teachers use features such as face recognition attendance, emotion detection, live class monitoring, and email OTP verification.

The system is designed to support learning engagement and attendance tracking within a controlled academic environment. MirrorMind does not perform any surveillance outside scheduled classroom sessions. All processing of biometric data requires explicit consent, and personal information is never sold or used for advertising. This policy reflects our commitment to transparency and data protection in an educational context.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

Full name, institutional email address (used for email OTP verification), role (student or teacher), and optional profile picture. This information is necessary for account creation, secure login, and role‑based access to classes.

2.2 Biometric Data

Facial recognition templates (mathematical representations known as “embeddings”) generated immediately before a student joins a live class. These are used solely for attendance verification. Raw video frames are processed in memory; only encrypted embeddings are retained. No facial images are permanently stored.

2.3 Classroom Analytics Data

During a live session, emotion indicators (e.g., attentive, confused, engaged) are derived from video streams using DeepFace / TensorFlow. These indicators are aggregated at the classroom level; individual identities are not included in the analytics dashboard. Teachers see only anonymised engagement trends, not personal emotional data.

2.4 Technical Data

Device type, browser version, IP address, camera permission status, and system logs. This information helps maintain security, troubleshoot issues, and ensure compatibility with the platform’s WebSocket and OpenCV components.

Explicit consent for biometrics: MirrorMind obtains opt‑in consent from students (or their guardians for minors) before any face recognition or emotion detection takes place. Consent may be withdrawn at any time through the institutional administrator.

3. How We Use Information

  • Authenticate users via email OTP and role‑based login.
  • Perform face matching to record attendance before class entry.
  • Generate real‑time, aggregated engagement metrics for teachers.
  • Maintain secure, encrypted biometric templates for the duration of the course.
  • Improve platform accuracy using de‑identified aggregate data.
  • Ensure compliance with academic integrity and security policies.

No commercial use: Personal and biometric data are used exclusively for educational analytics and attendance. MirrorMind does not sell, rent, or share any information with advertisers or third‑party marketing entities.

4. Biometric Data & Face Recognition Policy

MirrorMind’s use of facial recognition is strictly limited to attendance and classroom engagement analysis. Our specific commitments:

  • Face embeddings are encrypted (AES‑256) and stored separately from names or direct identifiers.
  • Raw video is processed ephemerally — it is never written to disk or retained after the session ends.
  • Emotion detection outputs are aggregated; no individual emotional profile is created or accessible.
  • Students cannot view other students’ analytics; only teachers and authorised institutional staff see aggregated class data.
  • Explicit consent is obtained before any biometric capture, and a clear opt‑out mechanism is available via the educational institution.
  • Biometric data is never used for surveillance, commercial research, or any purpose outside the defined academic features.

5. Data Storage & Security

All data in transit is encrypted using TLS 1.3. Stored data, including biometric embeddings and personal information, is encrypted at rest with AES‑256. The platform runs on secure academic infrastructure; access is restricted to authorised system administrators and institutional staff. Regular vulnerability assessments are conducted in line with academic project guidelines. Raw classroom video is processed in memory and not permanently recorded.

6. Data Sharing

MirrorMind limits data sharing to the following essential contexts:

  • With the educational institution that has deployed MirrorMind (teachers, administrators).
  • With subprocessors such as cloud hosting providers, who are bound by strict data protection terms.
  • If required to comply with a legal obligation or valid request from a public authority.
  • With explicit consent from the individual or their parent/guardian.

No biometric templates, video data, or personal information is shared with external commercial entities, advertisers, or non‑academic third parties.

7. User Rights

As a user of MirrorMind (student, teacher, or parent/guardian), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal and biometric data held about you.
  • Rectification: correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Erasure (right to be forgotten): request deletion of your data, including face embeddings, after course completion or at any time.
  • Restriction of processing: temporarily suspend data use while a request is evaluated.
  • Data portability: receive a structured, machine‑readable copy of your data (where technically feasible).
  • Withdrawal of consent: withdraw consent for biometric processing at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

To exercise these rights, please contact your institution’s MirrorMind administrator or email privacy@mirrormind.edu. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Data Retention

Personal information is retained while the user is enrolled in an active class or as required by the educational institution’s data retention schedule. Biometric face embeddings are automatically deleted within 30 days after:

  • the student’s course completion or withdrawal,
  • consent withdrawal by the student or guardian, or
  • termination of the institutional agreement.

Aggregated analytics (engagement statistics that do not identify individuals) may be retained longer for academic research and platform improvement, always in de‑identified form.

9. Children’s Privacy

MirrorMind is used in schools and may process data of students under the age of 13. We obtain verifiable parental or guardian consent before collecting any biometric information, in accordance with applicable laws (e.g., FERPA, GDPR‑K). Parents and guardians have the right to review their child’s data, request deletion, and withdraw consent. If you believe a child’s data has been collected without proper consent, please contact us immediately.

10. Changes to Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated to reflect changes in the platform or legal requirements. Material updates will be communicated via email to institutional administrators and through a notice on the login page. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this document indicates the most recent revision. Continued use of MirrorMind after such changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
